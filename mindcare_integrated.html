🫁 Breathing Exercises for Stress Relief

These evidence-based breathing techniques can help reduce anxiety, stress, and promote relaxation.

4-7-8 Breathing Technique

Perfect for anxiety and insomnia. Developed by Dr. Andrew Weil.

  1. Sit comfortably with your back straight
  2. Place the tip of your tongue against the tissue behind your upper front teeth
  3. Exhale completely through your mouth, making a whoosh sound
  4. Close your mouth and inhale through your nose for 4 counts
  5. Hold your breath for 7 counts
  6. Exhale through your mouth for 8 counts, making a whoosh sound
  7. Repeat the cycle 3-4 times

Box Breathing (4-4-4-4)

Used by Navy SEALs and athletes for focus and calm.

  1. Sit in a comfortable position
  2. Exhale slowly through your mouth
  3. Inhale through your nose for 4 counts
  4. Hold your breath for 4 counts
  5. Exhale through your mouth for 4 counts
  6. Hold empty for 4 counts
  7. Repeat for 5-10 cycles

💡 Quick Tips

  • Practice breathing exercises daily, not just during stress
  • Start with 5-10 minutes per session
  • Find a quiet, comfortable space
  • Don't force it - if you feel dizzy, take a break
  • Use apps like Breathwrk or Calm for guided sessions

🧘 Meditation Guide for Students

Simple meditation practices that fit into your busy student life.

Guided Meditation Timer

05:00

Basic Mindfulness Meditation

  1. Find a quiet spot and sit comfortably
  2. Close your eyes or soften your gaze
  3. Focus on your natural breath
  4. When thoughts arise, acknowledge them and return to breath
  5. Start with 5 minutes, gradually increase

💡 Meditation Tips for Students

  • Even 5 minutes daily is beneficial
  • Meditate at the same time each day to build a habit
  • Use meditation between study sessions
  • Try apps like Headspace, Calm, or Insight Timer
  • Join your campus meditation group if available

😴 Sleep Hygiene for Better Mental Health

Quality sleep is crucial for academic performance and mental wellbeing.

Sleep Schedule Optimization

  1. Go to bed and wake up at the same time every day
  2. Aim for 7-9 hours of sleep per night
  3. Avoid "catching up" on sleep during weekends
  4. Create a consistent bedtime routine

💡 Common Sleep Mistakes Students Make

  • Studying in bed (your bed should be for sleep only)
  • All-night cramming sessions
  • Excessive caffeine consumption
  • Irregular sleep schedules
  • Using phones/laptops in bed

📚 Managing Academic Stress

Proven strategies to handle study pressure and exam anxiety.

Time Management Strategies

  1. Use the Pomodoro Technique (25 min study + 5 min break)
  2. Create a weekly study schedule
  3. Break large assignments into smaller tasks
  4. Prioritize tasks using the Eisenhower Matrix
  5. Set realistic daily goals

💡 Academic Stress Busters

  • Exercise regularly to reduce stress hormones
  • Practice saying "no" to overcommitment
  • Celebrate small achievements
  • Remember: grades don't define your worth
  • Use campus resources like tutoring centers

👥 Overcoming Social Anxiety

Build confidence and improve social connections on campus.

Gradual Exposure Therapy

  1. Start with low-stakes interactions (asking for directions)
  2. Make eye contact and smile at people you pass
  3. Ask a question in class
  4. Join a club or organization
  5. Attend a campus social event
  6. Initiate conversations with classmates

💡 Social Confidence Builders

  • Join clubs aligned with your interests
  • Volunteer for causes you care about
  • Practice social skills in low-pressure environments
  • Remember that rejection isn't personal
  • Focus on being a good friend rather than being liked

  • Listen without judgment
  • Take all threats seriously
  • Don't promise to keep secrets about safety
  • Encourage professional help

  • Follow up and check in regularly
